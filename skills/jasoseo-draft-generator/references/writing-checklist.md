# Writing Checklist

## Map Evidence First

- Extract the core JD keywords and product context before writing.
- Match each major keyword to one concrete experience, project, or workflow from the background files.
- Reuse previous letters as evidence inventory and tone reference, not as copy-paste source.

## Draft Conservatively

- Keep the output in Korean business prose.
- Prefer grounded claims over inflated adjectives.
- Mention metrics only when they already exist in the source files.
- Treat company research as researched context, not as guaranteed internal truth.

## Default Structure

- Use explicit application questions when they are present in the source materials.
- Fall back to `지원동기`, `직무역량`, `입사 후 포부` when no official questions are available.
- Let each section answer one clear question: why this company, why this role, why this candidate now.

## Personalization Rules

- Connect the user's existing experience to the target company's current products, market direction, or engineering needs.
- Highlight one or two differentiating themes that recur across the user's materials.
- Rewrite recurring phrases when they sound too obviously borrowed from older letters.

## Final Checks

- Verify that the final filename follows `회사_직무_자소서초안.md`.
- Verify that the file lives in the target folder's `output/` directory.
- Remove unsupported speculation, repeated sentences, and generic closing lines.
